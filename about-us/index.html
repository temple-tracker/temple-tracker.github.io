Our Story

Every idea begins with a moment.

Vibin GuevaraFounder, Temple Tracker

For Temple Tracker, it did not begin inside an office or during a business discussion. It began quietly — inside temples, on small streets, and through conversations with ordinary people.

I have always believed temples are more than just places of worship. They are living centers of community, culture, emotion, and livelihood.

But over time, I started noticing a common problem everywhere I went — important temple events were happening, yet many people who genuinely wanted to be there simply never knew about them.

One incident in Bengaluru stayed with me deeply.

I was visiting a temple during a festival day when a beautifully decorated temple chariot procession was being pulled inside the temple streets. The atmosphere was vibrant and spiritual.

But while speaking to a few residents living just at the end of the same street, they told me they had no idea the event was happening. They said if they had known earlier, they would have come with their families.

Many temples conduct wonderful events, poojas, festivals, annadhanams, and processions — but there is no simple and accessible way for devotees to know about them on time.

Temple Tracker was born from that realization.

As I continued travelling to temples, I started observing another side of this ecosystem — the small street vendors around temples. Balloon sellers, flower vendors, pani poori stalls, cotton candy sellers, toy sellers, and many others depend heavily on temple gatherings for their daily income.

“If only we knew where special poojas or temple functions were happening, we could plan and sell there.”

That conversation changed the way I looked at temples entirely.

A tree does not grow alone.

When a plant becomes a tree, an entire ecosystem forms around it — birds, shade, insects, life, and shelter. In the same way, temples silently support an ecosystem of devotees, workers, vendors, traditions, and communities around them.

When temple events become visible and accessible to people, it does not only help devotees. It also helps the countless small vendors and families whose livelihoods are connected to temple activity.

That is why the Event System in Temple Tracker was created — to help temples share their events and help communities stay connected.

Timely information matters.

I have seen older parents travel for hours to visit a temple for a special pooja, only to discover that the pooja had ended long before they arrived because the timings were never updated properly anywhere. Their disappointment was heartbreaking.

In another incident, I saw a young couple travelling with a three-month-old baby to a temple area, unaware that nearby flooding had forced the temple to close temporarily. There was no alert, no notice, and no easy way for visitors to know the situation before starting their journey.

That is why Temple Tracker includes Alerts and Updates — so devotees can know about schedule changes, closures, crowd situations, or important announcements before they travel.

Preserving the Forgotten

But perhaps the most emotional reason behind this project came from seeing forgotten temples disappear slowly over time.

I came across old temples hidden deep inside streets, some not even marked on maps anymore. In certain places, rapid urban development and apartment expansion gradually consumed the surrounding temple lands. Over time, the original existence of those temples faded away from public memory itself.

That felt wrong to me. Even if a temple becomes abandoned, damaged, or hidden, its history should not disappear forever. Its existence deserves to be documented and remembered.

That belief led to the “Add Temple” feature in Temple Tracker — a way for people to digitally preserve temple information, history, locations, traditions, and identity for future generations.

More than just a project.

It is an attempt to connect devotees with temples more meaningfully, support local communities around temples, preserve forgotten heritage, and make spiritual journeys smoother and more informed for everyone.

If this platform helps even one elderly devotee reach a pooja on time, helps one small vendor earn a better day’s income, or helps preserve the memory of one forgotten temple — then this journey has already become meaningful.
